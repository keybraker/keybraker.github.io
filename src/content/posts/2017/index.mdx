In 2012, my 20th year, I wrote a quick <XA href="http://press10.tumblr.com/post/39331250761">blog post</XA> wrapping up some of my favorite things from what I called a 'jumbled and transitory' year. If 2012 was jumbled, I lack the vocabulary to describe this year. (Jumbled-er?) Exhausting and edifying might be close.

This year, despite being incredibly difficult, was my most creatively productive. I launched projects, both personal and professional, that I'm proud to talk about: <XA href="https://www.color-composer.net/">Color Composer</XA>, an interactive installation for the Nelson-Atkins' new <XA href="https://nelson-atkins.org/the-bloch-galleries/">Bloch Galleries</XA>; <XA href="https://medium.com/moonshotlab/finding-carmen-3c5e28c92f6b">Carmen</XA>, a chatbot made to help <XA href="https://www.barkleyus.com/">Barkley</XA> partners find their way; the Mapkins Machine, an Arduino-powered vending machine that dispenses branded napkins when sent a text; <XA href="https://rottentrumpatoes.com/">Rotten Trumpatoes</XA>, a silly website inspired by <XA href="https://twitter.com/PJVogt/status/895824447113842689">a tweet</XA>; <XA href="https://chrome.google.com/webstore/detail/they-linkedin/cmjockpmecfhkaeadcgneakffkioombe">They LinkedIn</XA>, a silly Chrome extension inspired by <XA href="https://trackchanges.postlight.com/paul-and-rich-try-to-talk-about-something-else-but-end-up-complaining-about-linkedin-again-c20526cfdb74">a podcast</XA>; this site (still a work in progress), my first made with the very promising <XA href="https://www.gatsbyjs.org/">Gatsby.js</XA>.

I also made significant steps towards bettering my health overall: I sought and heeded the aid of mental health professionals; I quit even 'casual' smoking entirely; I flossed more than all my previous years combined (gross, I know, sorry).

Still, 2017 was emotionally wrenching. I'll gloss over the details because I know this year was exhausting for nearly everyone, maybe save for those who get their news exclusively from Fox. In March my dearly loved childhood dog, <XA href="https://photos.google.com/share/AF1QipPXh0WAEJVOuE9Ki5Z0c0f4RkimbXjNKEaznuFkCtqEUifLpwd1SEhTE2AErrzVTQ/photo/AF1QipNjSOYCLbhITC-Cszg6-4LZcNeYkUjMWo8BDzkY?key=bkF0Q0FEeXJfcEdvQzgxd0FGTjk0dVZZMnRJN0tn">Keegan</XA>, passed away. I gained, then lost, relationships dear to me. My laundromat, staffed by a pair of lovely women who all but adopted me, who gave me leftover roast and sent me a Christmas card, abruptly closed. I probably drank too much.

Despite all that, there was plenty of good in 2017. I had the opportunity to travel to places I'd never been with people I love. I was enriched by the work of artists and creators I have the privilege to know. I ate a shit-ton of good food (excuse the expression).

Without further ado, here's a list, in no particular order, of a few of the things I enjoyed last year. Cheers!
